# Strategic Brand Discovery Questions

These questions are designed to extract the deep insights needed to build a comprehensive brand strategy report. Ask clients to answer thoughtfully and specifically—generic answers produce generic strategies.

---

## SECTION 1: BRAND ESSENCE & FOUNDATION

**1.1** What is your origin story? What problem or frustration led you to start this business? (Be specific about the pain point, not just a vague desire to help people)

**1.2** What is your core mission? What change do you want to create in the world? (This should be bigger than making money—what impact do you want?)

**1.3** What are your top 3-5 non-negotiable core values? (These are principles you will not compromise on, even if it costs you money)

**1.4** If your brand were a person, who would it be? Describe their personality, values, and how they interact with others. (Are they the wise mentor, the bold rebel, the nurturing guide, the innovative pioneer?)

---

## SECTION 2: TARGET AUDIENCE DEEP DIVE

**2.1** Describe your first ideal customer avatar in detail. Include their demographics, lifestyle, fears, and aspirations. (Give them a name, age, income, job title, and paint a vivid picture of their daily life)

**2.2** Describe your second ideal customer avatar. How is this person different from your first avatar? (What different pain points or desires do they have?)

**2.3** Describe your third ideal customer avatar. What makes them distinct? (This could be a different market segment, industry, or customer type)

**2.4** What are the deepest, most painful problems your ideal customers face? Go beyond surface-level issues. (What keeps them up at night? What are they embarrassed about? What are they desperately trying to solve?)

**2.5** What do your ideal customers truly desire? What transformation are they seeking? (What do they want to feel, have, and do? What would make them feel successful?)

**2.6** What language and phrases do your ideal customers use to describe their problems and desires? (What words resonate with them? What metaphors do they use?)

---

## SECTION 3: COMPETITIVE POSITIONING

**3.1** Who are your top 3 competitors? For each, describe their positioning, pricing, and messaging. (Include both direct competitors and indirect alternatives)

**3.2** What is your unfair advantage? What can you do that your competitors cannot easily replicate? (This could be your unique process, your story, your expertise, your network, or your community)

**3.3** What gap exists in your market that you uniquely fill? (What are customers frustrated about with current solutions? How do you solve that differently?)

**3.4** Why should a customer choose you over your competitors? Give 3-5 compelling reasons. (Be specific and differentiated—not generic claims like 'great service' or 'affordable')

---

## SECTION 4: TRANSFORMATION PROMISE

**4.1** What is the 'before' state? Describe your customer's situation before they work with you. (Paint a vivid picture of their frustration, struggle, or limitation)

**4.2** What is the 'after' state? Describe the transformation your customer experiences after working with you. (Be specific about results, feelings, and changes in their life or business)

**4.3** What is the journey your customer takes from 'before' to 'after'? What are the key milestones? (Map out the steps, phases, or stages of their transformation)

**4.4** How long does the transformation typically take? What are realistic timelines for results? (Be honest about what's possible in 30 days, 90 days, 6 months, etc.)

**4.5** How do you measure success? What specific metrics or outcomes prove your transformation works? (Revenue increase, time saved, emotional shift, skill gained, etc.)

---

## SECTION 5: OFFER ARCHITECTURE & PRICING

**5.1** What are your current offers or service tiers? Describe what each includes and the price. (Include entry-level, mid-tier, and premium offerings if you have them)

**5.2** What is your ideal offer structure? How would you tier your offerings if you could start fresh? (Consider DIY, Done-with-you, Done-for-you, or other models)

**5.3** What is your pricing logic? Why are your prices what they are? (Is it based on value delivered, time invested, market positioning, or something else?)

**5.4** How do you move customers up your offer ladder? What's the progression from entry-level to premium? (How do you upsell or cross-sell?)

**5.5** What high-value bonuses or add-ons do you include with each tier? (What makes each offer a 'no-brainer'?)

---

## SECTION 6: MARKET OPPORTUNITY & TIMING

**6.1** Why is NOW the perfect time for your brand? What trends or shifts are creating this opportunity? (What has changed in the market, technology, or customer behavior?)

**6.2** What is the size of your target market? How many potential customers are there? (Provide estimates or research if available)

**6.3** Is your market growing? What is the growth trajectory? (Is demand increasing, stable, or decreasing?)

**6.4** What is your vision for your business in 1 year, 3 years, and 5 years? (Revenue goals, customer base, market position, impact)

---

## SECTION 7: MESSAGING & POSITIONING

**7.1** What is your core message? If you could only say ONE thing about your brand, what would it be? (This should be powerful, specific, and differentiated)

**7.2** What are your 3-5 messaging pillars? What key ideas do you want to communicate? (These should align with your target audience's pain points and desires)

**7.3** What is your brand voice? How do you speak to your audience? (Professional, playful, authoritative, approachable, bold, nurturing?)

**7.4** What are the main objections your customers have? How do you overcome them? (Too expensive, not enough time, won't work for me, etc.)

---

## SECTION 8: CONTENT & MARKETING STRATEGY

**8.1** What are your 3-5 content pillars? What topics will you create content around? (These should educate, inspire, or solve problems for your audience)

**8.2** What are your primary marketing channels? Where does your audience hang out? (Social media, email, blog, podcast, YouTube, LinkedIn, etc.)

**8.3** What content formats work best for you? (Blog posts, videos, podcasts, webinars, etc.) (What do you enjoy creating and what resonates with your audience?)

**8.4** How do you generate leads? What is your lead magnet or entry point? (Free challenge, email course, webinar, assessment, etc.)

---

## SECTION 9: BUSINESS MODEL & REVENUE

**9.1** What are your revenue streams? How do you make money? (Services, products, courses, memberships, affiliate, etc.)

**9.2** What is your ideal revenue model? How would you structure your business for maximum profitability? (High-ticket services, scalable products, recurring revenue, etc.)

**9.3** What is the average customer lifetime value? How much does a customer spend with you over time? (Include initial purchase + upsells + repeat business)

**9.4** What are your key conversion metrics? (Website visitors to leads, leads to customers, etc.) (What percentage of people move through your funnel?)

---

## SECTION 10: DIFFERENTIATION & UNFAIR ADVANTAGE

**10.1** Do you have a unique methodology, framework, or process? Describe it. (This could be proprietary, trademarked, or simply your unique approach)

**10.2** What is your unique story? What experiences or background give you credibility? (Your journey, transformation, or unique perspective)

**10.3** Do you have a unique community, network, or audience? What makes it special? (Your followers, members, alumni, or tribe)

**10.4** What results or outcomes do you deliver that competitors don't? (Be specific about what makes your results different or better)

---

## INSTRUCTIONS FOR CLIENTS

Answer each question thoughtfully and specifically. The more detailed and honest your answers, the more comprehensive and valuable your brand strategy report will be. Don't rush—take time to think deeply about each question.

These questions will populate a comprehensive 60-70 page brand strategy report that covers:
- Your brand essence and competitive positioning
- Deep-dive analysis of your ideal customers
- Your transformation promise and value proposition
- Strategic offer architecture and pricing
- Messaging and positioning strategy
- Content and marketing strategy
- Business model and revenue optimization
- Your unfair advantage and differentiation
- 90-day action plan
- Competitive analysis

Your answers are the foundation. The report is the strategy. The implementation is the results.
